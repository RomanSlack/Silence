## Data collection and analysis

### Session 2026-05-02_19-35-21 (first session, no pressing, said words aloud)
- 9 words × 1 trial each initially, grew to ~4 trials per word for first 10 words
- Words "left" through "five": both channels 400–1400 µV simultaneously — artifact,
  not EMG. Electrode contact failed mid-session.
- Clean words (yes–down): CH1 ~132 µV, CH2 30–40 µV
- CH2 barely above resting baseline — orbicularis contact was weak

### Session 2026-05-02_20-05-03 (5 words, pressing hard with fingers)
- 5 trials × 5 words, said aloud, pressed electrodes with fingers throughout
- CH1 RMS jumped to 1200–1700 µV (was 130 µV) — pressing artifacts
- Trial-to-trial std ~750 µV on CH1, ~430 µV on CH2 (CV ~50%)
- LOO SVM accuracy: 0% on CH1 only, CH2 only, and both combined
- Conclusion: pressing introduces movement artifacts larger than the speech signal

### quick_classify.py (new script)
LOO cross-validation with RBF SVM. Extracts per-channel features:
- RMS per quarter-window (4 × temporal segments)
- Global RMS, MAV, waveform length, zero crossing rate
- Runs on most recent session by default, or pass session path + word list

### plot_session.py (new script)
Plots filtered CH1 + CH2 waveforms for every trial in a session,
one row per trial, saved to `emg_overview.png` in the session dir.

## Key findings

1. **CH1 (masseter) is not word-discriminative for overt speech.** It fires for
   every word because all speech involves jaw opening/closing. Same RMS and
   waveform shape across yes/no/stop/go/start. Useful for onset detection and
   as a context channel, not for word identity.

2. **CH2 (orbicularis oris) is the discriminative channel.** Different words
   show visibly different waveform shapes and timing — "yes", "no", "stop"
   looked distinct on the plot. This is the expected result: lip shape is
   phoneme-specific, jaw movement is not.

3. **Electrode adhesion is the blocker.** Without pressing: contact too weak
   (CH2 barely above baseline). With pressing: artifacts swamp the signal.
   Gold cups + medical tape are inadequate for a moving face. Options:
   - Stronger tape (Nexcare/3M Transpore) over the top of the cup
   - Small pediatric Ag/AgCl snap electrodes (24mm diameter)
