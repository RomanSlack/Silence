# Session Log — 2026-04-21 (Hardware bring-up)

## Goal
Get the OpenBCI V3 clone (wired/serial version) talking to the PC for the first time.

## What we learned about the hardware

- **Board**: OpenBCI V3 clone, PIC32 (32-bit), firmware **v3.1.1** (pre-burned by seller)
- **Connection type**: wired serial — NOT wireless/dongle. The "dongle" is a CP2102 USB-to-UART cable with 3 flying leads (white, green, black = RX, TX, GND)
- **BLE1 header**: the seller replaced the original RFduino BLE radio with a 3-pin female header at the `BLE1` position on the board. This is the serial connection point.
- **Correct wiring orientation**: GND (black wire) → **north pin** of BLE1 header. Confirmed by red LED lighting up — the LED was the most reliable diagnostic, not the multimeter (which had a dead battery).
- **USB serial chip**: CP2102 (Silicon Labs). Enumerates as `/dev/ttyUSB0`.
- **GUI compatibility**: OpenBCI GUI ≥5.x won't recognize CP2102 (FTDI-only). We skip the GUI entirely — BrainFlow handles everything.

## Electrode pinout (top/north header, left to right)

```
BIAS  N8P  N7P  N6P  N5P  N4P  N3P  N2P  N1P  SRB
```

**MVP wiring (top row only, shared-reference mode):**
- N1P ← mentalis (chin, below lower lip)
- N2P ← masseter (jaw hinge — clench to find the bulge)
- SRB ← reference (mastoid — bony bump behind ear)
- BIAS ← bias/ground (same mastoid area, adjacent)
- Bottom row (N1N–N8N): leave unwired — internally shorted to SRB2 by firmware default

## Electrode signal check results

Signal reading key:
- **187,500 µV** = floating/disconnected — ADC rails to max, no skin contact or wire not seated
- **Staircase pattern** = 60Hz powerline pickup — cable acting as antenna, some contact but impedance too high for EMG
- **Near zero + small ripple** = good contact, ready for EMG
- **±2,000–5,000 µV spike on clench** = actual EMG burst — what we're looking for

Ch1 (mentalis, N1P): showed staircase = some electrical contact, 60Hz antenna pickup
Ch2 (masseter, N2P): stuck at 187,500 = floating, no skin contact

Attempted dry gold cups → failed. Gold cups have rim-only skin contact when dry — designed to hold gel.
Attempted facial moisturizer as improvised conductor → temporarily dropped ch2 from 187,500 to ~120,000–140,000, proving signal chain is alive end-to-end. Evaporated within ~2 minutes, back to floating.

**Conclusion**: signal chain confirmed working. Blocker is electrode conductivity — need proper gel.

## Blocker: need electrode gel

## Troubleshooting mental model (built today)

Always isolate one link at a time. The chain is:
**skin → electrode → DuPont wire → board pin → ADS1299 → BrainFlow → script**

Diagnostic sequence used today:
1. Does the USB cable enumerate? (`lsusb`, `ls /dev/ttyUSB*`)
2. Does the board respond to serial commands? (`serial_probe.py` — send `v`, listen for firmware string)
3. Does BrainFlow connect and stream? (`cyton_connect.py`)
4. Do electrodes show signal? (`signal_viz.py` — watch for drop from 187,500)

LED indicator on board is more reliable than a multimeter for wiring orientation — remember this.
