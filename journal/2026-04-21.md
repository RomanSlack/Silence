# Session Log — 2026-04-21 (Hardware bring-up)

## Goal
Get the OpenBCI V3 clone (wired/serial version) talking to the PC for the first time.

## What we learned about the hardware

- **Board**: OpenBCI V3 clone, PIC32 (32-bit), firmware **v3.1.1** (pre-burned by seller)
- **Connection type**: wired serial — NOT wireless/dongle. The "dongle" is a CP2102 USB-to-UART cable with 3 flying leads (white, green, black = RX, TX, GND)
- **BLE1 header**: the seller replaced the original RFduino BLE radio with a 3-pin female header at the `BLE1` position on the board. This is the serial connection point.
- **Correct wiring orientation**: GND (black wire) → **north pin** of BLE1 header. This was confirmed by a red LED lighting up on the board in this orientation. White/green carry TX/RX in the other two positions.
- **USB serial chip**: CP2102 (Silicon Labs). Enumerates as `/dev/ttyUSB0`.
- **GUI compatibility**: OpenBCI GUI ≥5.x won't recognize CP2102 (FTDI-only). Use GUI 4.x or skip GUI entirely (we skip it — BrainFlow handles everything).

## What landed
