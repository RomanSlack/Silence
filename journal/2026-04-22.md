# Session Log — 2026-04-22 (Clone BIAS/SRB is dead — bipolar mode is the way)

## Goal
Spectra 360 gel arrived. Get clean EMG signal on masseter with gel, then
start Session A (500 overt trials).

## What we actually did: hours of electrode debugging that led to a fundamental
## hardware discovery about this clone board.

## Symptom progression

1. Gelled N2P (masseter), BIAS, SRB on face properly (cup well filled, alcohol
   prep, tape). CH2 rails at +187,500 µV. CH1 staircases (60 Hz pickup).
2. Swapped N1P ↔ N2P wires at the board header — no change. Rules out the
   electrode/wire on the masseter side.
3. Reseated BIAS and SRB, checked crimps, checked pin orientation (BIAS is
   leftmost, SRB is rightmost of top header — user is reading silkscreen
   correctly). No change.
4. Wrist test: electrode in air drifts off rail, on skin instantly rails to
   +187,500. Classic symptom of BIAS loop being open — body has no defined
   DC potential relative to the ADC.
5. Moved *all three* (BIAS, SRB, N3P active) to inner forearm — cleanest skin,
   no hair, thin. Active channel *still* rails. Proves head prep is not the
   issue. Something is structurally broken in the BIAS/SRB path.
6. Modified `signal_viz.py` to show all 8 channels — all 8 rail identically
   at +187,500 (solid, not flickering). Consistent with shared-reference
   (BIAS/SRB) failure: every channel depends on it the same way, so all
   fail the same way.

## Register dump (scripts/dump_registers.py, new)

Dumped ADS1299 register state via the Cyton `?` serial command. All registers
match the standard OpenBCI defaults:

- `CONFIG3 = 0xEC` — `PD_BIAS=1` (bias buffer enabled), `BIASREF_INT=1`
  (mid-supply reference), `BIAS_STAT=0` (**chip reports BIAS electrode is
  connected**).
- `BIAS_SENSP = BIAS_SENSN = 0xFF` — all 8 channels feed the BIAS_DRV loop.
- `CHnSET = 0x68` — gain 24x, normal input, SRB2 routed.
- `MISC1 = 0x00` — SRB2 mode (standard Cyton default).

So the firmware is configured correctly, and the chip *thinks* BIAS is
working. Something between the ADS1299 pins and the silkscreened `BIAS`/`SRB`
pins on the header is wrong.

## Shorted-input self-test (scripts/ch_selftest.py, new)

Set CH1 input MUX to SHORTED (MUX bits = 001), internally shorting the PGA
inputs together. Bypasses electrodes, wires, BIAS, SRB — purely tests the
amp chain.

```
CH1 SHORTED-input result:
  min  = -22.2 uV
  max  = 0.0 uV
  mean = -21.5 uV
  std  = 0.6 uV

=> CLEAN. Amp is healthy. The fault is in external path.
```

Textbook healthy ADS1299 (sub-µV noise floor). **The chip, ADC, PGA, and
analog supply are all fine. The clone's header silkscreen is lying:
either the `BIAS` pin is not connected to BIAS_DRV, or the `SRB` pin is not
connected to SRB2, or both.**

## Bipolar workaround (scripts/bipolar_test.py, new)

If BIAS/SRB are broken on this clone, we don't use them. Instead, operate
each channel in **bipolar differential mode**: two electrodes per channel,
one to N*P (top row) and one to N*N (bottom row, directly underneath),
with CHnSET bits set to disconnect SRB2 and exclude the channel from the
BIAS loop.

Channel setting command: `x1060000X`
  x=config, 1=ch1, P=0 on, G=6 gain24, T=0 normal, bias=0, SRB2=0, SRB1=0

Wired two gel cups on forearm ~2 cm apart on the muscle belly, one to N1P
and one to N1N. **IT WORKED.**

Readings (bipolar_test.py output):
- Baseline DC offset: mean ~−41,000 µV (not railed — 187,500 is the rail,
  plenty of headroom; the offset is just the undriven body potential and
  will be filtered out by the 20–500 Hz bandpass).
- Rest rows: std ~300–400 µV, range ~1000 µV.
- Clench rows: std jumps to 500–1000 µV, range 2000–4000 µV. Clear EMG
  bursts riding on the DC offset.

The DC offset is cosmetic. First preprocessing step in the ML pipeline is
a bandpass filter, which strips it automatically.

## Implications for the project

- **Clone's BIAS and SRB are unusable.** Not a setup issue — the clone board's
  header routes these pins to something other than the ADS1299's BIAS_DRV /
  SRB2. Don't try to fix it. Don't rely on a mastoid reference.
- **4 working channels, not 8.** Each bipolar pair uses both N*P and N*N,
  so 8 analog inputs become 4 differential channels.
- **MVP needs only 2 channels** (mentalis + masseter), which is **4 electrodes
  total** — doable with current gold cups.
- **Electrode placement for EMG**: two electrodes along the muscle belly,
  ~2 cm apart, running parallel to the muscle fibers. No mastoid reference
  needed.
- **No hair/oil debugging behind the ear.** Entire mastoid prep nightmare is
  moot — those cups don't need to be there.

## Lesson for future clone-board hardware

When every channel fails identically, suspect the shared path (BIAS/SRB)
before suspecting individual electrodes or prep. The shorted-input MUX test
is the fastest way to prove amp health — it takes the entire external path
out of the equation in 5 seconds.

## Electrode placement decision (Roman-specific, face anatomy)

Roman has a soul patch (hair tuft on midline between lower lip and chin) —
exactly where the textbook mentalis placement sits. Gold cups can't bridge
gel through hair reliably (same failure mode as the mastoid behind-the-ear
debugging earlier today). Not shaving.

**Chosen placement (option 3 of three considered):**

- **CH1 — Masseter** (N1P + N1N). Unchanged. Pair ~2 cm apart along the
  muscle fibers (roughly vertical), over the belly of the bulge that pops
  out when biting down. Just in front of the earlobe at the jaw angle.
- **CH2 — Orbicularis oris** (N2P + N2N). Placed just **below one corner of
  the lower lip** (pick a side and stick with it for consistency). Pair
  ~1.5 cm apart. Captures the ring muscle around the mouth, which fires
  strongly during silent mouthing of any word with lip movement
  ("puh", "fuh", "no", "you"). Sidesteps the soul patch entirely.
  Arguably *better* than mentalis for silent-speech discrimination anyway.

**Why orbicularis oris over two discarded alternatives:**
- Option 1 (mentalis, pair moved below the soul patch toward the chin point):
  still on the midline, still near the hair, weaker signal from lower-fiber
  placement.
- Option 2 (mentalis, pair moved laterally off-center of the midline): works
  but mentalis is small and off-belly placement is weaker.
- Option 3 (orbicularis oris under lip corner): bare skin, strong signal
  for speech, no hair risk. Winner.

**Calibration discipline going forward**: mark the two spots with a
washable pen or snap a reference photo before taking cups off at the end
of each session so the next session's placement is identical. Consistency
across trials matters more than textbook muscle identity — the classifier
learns whatever signal differs between words, regardless of which muscle
name is on the anatomy chart.
